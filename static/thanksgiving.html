  • Meriel's Famous Dressing
    Combine breads in large bowl or pot.
Fry bacon and remove.
Fry sausage in same pan.
Saute onion and celery in same pan. I add the celery after the onion is
already partially sauteed so it retains a bit of crunch.
Mix everything with the bread. Start with the smaller amount of each herb
at this point.
Add butter and mix. Taste again and consider adding more herbs to your
taste. Hint: you should almost certainly add some more herbs.
Add chicken stock to moisten (1-3 Cups). Taste again and consider adding
more herbs. Sage is really the key herb here IMHO.
30 minutes in oven at 350, covered in foil. Microwave is ok too.
Best if made up to 1 day ahead so herbs and spices obtain their full flavor
and harmony. You can then either microwave it before serving or reheat it
in the oven. It really makes little to no difference. If you heat it in the
oven, you might want to add a bit more chicken broth to make sure it stays
moist.

  • Top Reddit 'Savory Stuffing'
    Dry the bread on two rimmed baking sheets in a 200° oven. Stir and rotate
pans every 15 minutes until done.
Meanwhile, in a dutch oven over medium heat, melt butter. Add celery. Cook,
stirring occasionally, 4 to 6 minutes. Add onions; cook until tender, about
6 to 9 minutes more. Add garlic and sauté for a minute. Transfer mixture to
a large bowl.
To celery-onion mixture, add bread, sage, parsley, pecans, and chestnuts.
Season with salt and pepper; toss to combine. Whisk together eggs, 2 cups
of stock, and cream. Add to bread mixture and toss to combine. Continue
adding stock and tossing just until stuffing is moistened but not.
To stuff the bird: Microwave about 5 cups of stuffing for about 2-3 minutes
on high, until it registers 120–130 degrees F. on an instant-read
thermometer. Stuff it in both cavities of the turkey.
Spoon remaining stuffing (or all of it) into buttered baking dish; cover
with buttered aluminum foil and refrigerate. When turkey is removed from
oven, place covered baking dish in oven, and bake at 375 degrees until
warmed through, about 45 minutes. Uncover and bake until golden, about 15
minutes more.
I tinker with this every year (hence the name). I've put golden raisins and
chopped apples in it some years. This year I'm leaving out the chestnuts.
Here’s a trick someone posted here a couple of weeks ago—I’m trying it this
year:
Place a cooking rack or broiler pan rack atop the dish in which you will
bake the dressing. Place a few chicken wings or turkey wings on the rack.
The juices dripping down will baste the dressing and make it taste more
like it was cooked inside the bird.
